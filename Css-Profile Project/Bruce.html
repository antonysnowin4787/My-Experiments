On Discipline

Bruce Wayne trained for years across the world — in silence, in pain, in obscurity — before ever putting on the mask. No shortcuts. No audience. Pure discipline. He understood that becoming Batman wasn't an event; it was a decade of daily choices.

On Consistency

Every night, regardless of injury or grief, Bruce showed up. Gotham didn't take nights off, and neither did he. True consistency means showing up when it's hard, when no one is watching, and when the results aren't visible yet.
