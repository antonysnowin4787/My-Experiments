On Discipline

Tony Stark didn't build Iron Man in a day — he built it in a cave with scraps. His discipline wasn't about perfect conditions; it was about using whatever he had. He iterated endlessly, failing fast, fixing faster, never waiting for the right moment.

On Consistency

Every suit was better than the last. Mark I to Mark L — each version was the result of consistent refinement. Tony didn't rest on success. He kept building, kept improving, driven by the belief that there was always a better version possible.
